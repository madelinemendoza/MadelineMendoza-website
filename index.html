I currently work as a cashier at a small family-owned business. The business is a specialty Market. We hold specialty items that no other stores in the nearby areas carry. Some examples of this are rare taco sauce and beers that cannot be purchased within a 100 mile radius. Just knowing these facts alone it is evident that my bosses have very great marketing skills. By holding specialty items that customers will drive for, they have made a business out of a regular mini grocery store. Our grocery store has very high prices and are considered to be convenience store prices however because we have specialty items customers tend to still buy regular items that are priced $2 over the regular price. The location of the market is also a very smart marketing tactic. Positioned in Redlands, California with mostly Elders surrounding it in the nearby vicinity, our Market is a prominent and key part of our city. Elders in the nearby houses are able to drive to only one store, as driving is too much for older people. Because these customers do not want to drive to other stores they end up buying everything they need at our specialty Market, meaning my boss makes a ridiculous amount of profit on almost every item they buy. To make matters even more convenient for my boss, the business is located down the street from a middle school. This Middle School is walking distance and has bad parking there for little kids in the Middle School come down to this Market Daly and buy sodas and candy. These little kids line up at the door and wait to be let in just a couple at a time just to buy sodas and candy. In return parents are able to pick their kids up in safe parking facilities. Not only location wise and item wise are my bosses smart but they also Have a very unique marketing tactics once you get inside of the store. One of the tactics I can recall is a $0.01 wine sale. This sale is where you buy one bottle of wine you can get your second bottle for $0.01 however you have to buy two of the same bottle. We profit off of this because the price of one bottle is actually the price of 2 bottles and while customers think they are buying only one bottle they end up paying for both without believing that they are. In return if the wine is good and they've gotten a so-called good deal on it they purchase it again. More tactics like this are used around the store for example buy one get one free deals on items like chickens and fresh meats. The market has also furthered its marketing strategies by adding in fun sections of the store over the years for example our beer section is actually a beer cave with fluorescent beer decorations and a cool cave you can walk into to buy our beer. We also have a small coffee bar where 2 employees work and serve coffee.
